Basotho Dances & Instruments

1. Mohobelo

A ceremonial dance performed by men at important community events. It features group footwork, synchronized steps, and call-and-response singing.

2. Mokhibo

Traditional dance performed by women and girls, characterized by rhythmic movement of shoulders and neck while kneeling or seated.

3. Mokorotlo

A male dance and song performed at significant events such as meetings and initiation ceremonies.

4. Setolo-tolo

A small mouth-resonated instrument played by rubbing or plucking. Produces rhythmic tones used to accompany songs.

5. Sekhankula

A single-stringed instrument made from a tin and wire, often played by young boys herding cattle.

6. Moropa

A small clay drum shaped like a pot with a spout, covered by animal hide. It is played by women at girls' initiation ceremonies.

7. Lekolulo

A type of traditional flute played by herdboys.

8. Litolobonya

A song and dance performed by girls and married women — girls perform it in public while women perform it in secret at post-childbirth celebrations called pitiki.

9. Pilane

A vigorous dance with stamping footwork; historically performed by young men demonstrating strength and agility.

10. Liphala

Brass bands and music influenced in the 19th century by British military bands.

11. Famo

A Sesotho music genre that involves the use of accordion and oil-can drums.

12. Thomo

A mouth-resonated traditional instrument played by women.

13. Wedding Dances

Joyful dances performed at weddings, featuring family participation and symbolic movements celebrating partnership.

14. Harvest Dances

Dances marking seasonal harvests, often accompanied by songs of thanks and shared communal meals.

15. Lekoko

A hardened roll of cowhide that produces a deep, thumping sound when beaten with sticks.

16. Pitiki Songs

Songs sung by women after a baby is born to celebrate the mother and child.

17. Initiation & Rite Dances

Dances associated with initiation ceremonies where young people are welcomed into adulthood.

18. Sekupu

A double-headed drum played by the Basotho, made from animal hide.

19. Footwork Dances

Dances that emphasize intricate foot patterns; often used in competitions and displays of skill.

20. Healing Songs

Music and chants used in healing rites to restore balance and call upon ancestral guidance.

21. Koriana

An instrument introduced through contact with European miners; used when performing Famo music.

22. Lelingoana

Hymns sung during initiation ceremonies.

23. Lesiba

A Basotho instrument made from a quill, sinew, and a piece of wood.
